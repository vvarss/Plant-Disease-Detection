Plant Diseases

The tomato leaves are classified into Healthy, Early Blight, and Late Blight identification.

Early Blight

Early blight is a common and serious fungal disease of tomato caused by Alternaria solani.

It typically starts on the lower leaves, producing dark spots with concentric rings and yellow halos. These lesions lead to leaf drop, reducing photosynthesis and yield.

The disease spreads through spores in warm, humid conditions and can persist in soil and debris. Effective management involves crop rotation, sanitation, and timely application of fungicides.

Favourable Conditions

  • Warm temperatures (24–29°C)
  • High humidity

Control

  • Crop rotation
  • Removal of infected debris
  • Fungicide sprays (e.g., mancozeb, chlorothalonil)

Late Blight

Late blight, caused by Phytophthora infestans, is a devastating disease of tomatoes, capable of wiping out entire crops under cool and moist conditions.

It causes rapidly expanding water-soaked lesions on leaves and stems, often with white spore masses under the leaves. Fruits develop brown, firm rot.

This pathogen spreads quickly and requires prompt action, including destruction of infected plants and use of systemic fungicides. Resistance breeding and weather-based forecasting are key strategies for control.

Favourable Conditions

  • Cool, wet weather (15–20°C, high humidity)

Control

  • Use of resistant varieties
  • Fungicide sprays (e.g., metalaxyl, cymoxanil)
  • Destroy infected plants immediately
